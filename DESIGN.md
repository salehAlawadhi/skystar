## Overview

Sky Star should feel like a custom-built Saudi industrial company website: operational, grounded, premium, and technically calm. The visual language is cinematic but not decorative. Every section should answer a client question: what is supplied, why trust it, how operations are coordinated, and how to request supply.

## Colors

Use deep navy as the foundation, steel gray for technical support text, white and mist for breathing room, and accent teal/blue only for direction and action. Sand should appear sparingly for desert and field context. Avoid purple, bright neon, and generic gradients.

## Typography

Arabic is the primary visual language. Use strong Arabic headings with generous line-height, no negative letter spacing, and compact but readable body copy. English pages should preserve the same hierarchy without becoming smaller or weaker.

## Layout

Alternate section types: cinematic hero, dense trust strip, editorial split image/text, tabbed service selector, full-screen journey, operational image stack, sector tiles, and strong final CTA. Avoid repeating identical three-card sections. Mobile must be designed as its own composition, not a squeezed desktop.

## Elevation & Depth

Depth should come from real photography, matte overlays, precise shadows, and subtle industrial grid lines. Do not use floating decorative blobs as the main visual idea. Use angled panels only when they reinforce movement, transport, or field operation.

## Shapes

Use restrained radii from 8px to 22px. Large rounded cards are allowed for CTA and media frames only. Avoid nested cards unless the inner card is a purposeful operational label.

## Components

Buttons must be clear and action-led. Service cards should feel operational, not SaaS-template generic. Footer must act as a final conversion section, not a link dump.

## Do's and Don'ts

Do use real tanker, fuel-storage, industrial, and field-operation imagery. Do preserve Arabic RTL balance. Do make CTAs visible in every major decision zone.

Do not claim certifications, GPS tracking, delivery times, or official partnerships unless confirmed by the client. Do not make the 3D journey look like a game. Do not over-animate text or delay critical content.
